# This Week's Company FAQ Digest

*Question*: When does the new hybrid work schedule start, and does it apply to everyone?
*Answer*: Starting September 1, 2026, employees move to a hybrid schedule — in office Tuesday through Thursday and remote on Monday and Friday. This applies company-wide unless your manager has an approved fully-remote exception on file with the People team. (Source: the People Team's Updated Hybrid Work Policy email.)

*Question*: Now that we have a new CFO, is anything changing about how budget approvals work?
*Answer*: Jordan Kim joins as CFO effective July 15, 2026, reporting directly to the CEO. There are no changes yet to how budget approvals or purchase requests work — Jordan will meet with team leads first, and any process changes will be announced company-wide before they take effect. (Source: the CEO's welcome email introducing Jordan Kim.)

*Question*: Is the Atlas launch still happening on the original August timeline?
*Answer*: No — the Atlas public launch has moved from August 2026 to October 2026 because our LIDAR sensor supplier is running about eight weeks behind on a key component. Nothing else about the launch plan (feature set, go-to-market approach) is changing. (Source: the PM Team's Atlas Launch Timeline Update email.)

*Question*: With the Atlas launch delay, should we be worried about a hiring freeze or layoffs?
*Answer*: No decision has been made — at the June 30 all-hands, the CEO said leadership is watching the timeline closely but has nothing to announce, and will communicate directly if that changes. Treat this as an open question rather than a confirmed outcome until an official update goes out. (Source: the June 30 all-hands notes.)
