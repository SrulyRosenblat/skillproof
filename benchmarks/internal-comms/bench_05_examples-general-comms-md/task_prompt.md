# Task: Draft an internal company announcement

You work in internal communications at Example Corp. Platform Engineering has
asked you to write a one-off internal announcement. It isn't a weekly
progress update, a company newsletter, or an FAQ — just a standalone
heads-up that needs to go out to a specific audience.

Source information has been gathered in `/workspace/facts.md`. It's a raw,
unordered dump of notes from the planning thread — not every line carries
equal weight, and it includes at least one detail that isn't relevant to
this particular announcement.

You've also been given `/workspace/style_examples.md`: two announcements
Platform Engineering / IT Security have sent out in the past. Whoever reads
your draft will expect it to look and read like those did.

Here's the brief for this announcement, already confirmed with the
requester:

- **Audience**: every employee who has a DevPortal account
- **Purpose**: make sure they understand the one thing they're required to
  do, why, and what happens if they don't
- **Tone**: informational and direct — this is a mandatory heads-up, not a
  celebratory or alarming one
- **Format**: match the structure and conventions demonstrated by both
  examples in `style_examples.md`

## Output

Write your announcement to `/workspace/announcement.md`. Requirements:

- Written for the audience, purpose, and tone described above
- Follows the same structural conventions demonstrated by both examples in
  `style_examples.md`
- References the two links given in `facts.md`, so the reader can act
  immediately and knows where to ask questions
- Signed off the way the examples are, attributed to the team named in
  `facts.md` as the rollout owner
- Draws only on the facts that are actually relevant to what this audience
  needs to do and know — don't pad it with unrelated information from the
  notes

Do not create any files other than `/workspace/announcement.md`.
