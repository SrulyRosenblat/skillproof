# Design Brief: Aria launch page

Client: Aria Machine Co.
Deliverable: a single-page site announcing a new product.

## The product

- Name: **Aria**
- Category: a tabletop, fully manual lever espresso machine — no pump, no PID
  controller. The user pulls a spring lever by hand to build pressure.
- Peak pressure: **9 bar**, reached entirely by the strength and timing of the
  person pulling the lever.
- Boiler: passive thermosiphon, no electronic temperature control.
- Materials: brushed brass fittings, a matte black powder-coated steel body,
  and a **walnut** lever handle shaped to fit the palm.
- Price: $2,400.
- Run size: a limited run of 200 units, hand-assembled, shipping Q3.

## Audience

Home baristas who already own a grinder and want full manual control over
the shot — people who find fully automatic machines uninteresting because
the machine does the work instead of the person.

## Voice

The company's existing materials are matter-of-fact and unhurried: they talk
about spring tension, palm shape, and thermosiphon flow the way a watchmaker
talks about a movement. No hype language, no exclamation points, no "game
changer" or "revolutionary."

## What the page must do

Announce Aria to this audience, explain what makes the manual lever
mechanism worth $2,400 compared to an automatic machine, and give them a way
to get on the waiting list for the limited run (a simple form or mailto
link is enough — no backend is required).

No colors, fonts, or layout are specified — that part is left to the
designer.
