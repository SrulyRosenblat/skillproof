# Self-critique — Aria

- Color: REVISE — cream, charcoal and terracotta is the same warm-neutral-plus-terracotta trio that fits almost any craft brief and says nothing about a machine built from brass, black steel and walnut, so it's replaced with a palette pulled from the machine's own materials instead.
- Type: KEEP — Playfair Display already reads like foundry lettering stamped on a brass nameplate and Source Sans Pro stays out of the way of the spec-heavy body copy, which is exactly the register a hand-built lever machine calls for.
- Layout: KEEP — hero, then a feature grid, then a waitlist band is the right shape for this brief: one clear headline, room to explain why manual pressure beats a pump, then a low-friction way to sign up.
- Signature: REVISE — a static gauge illustration shows the number 9 bar without showing what makes the machine different, so it's replaced with a lever the visitor pulls themselves, putting the actual hand-pulled mechanism the brief is about into the visitor's hand instead of just illustrating a spec.

## Notes

The color and signature choices were the two that would have looked the same
regardless of what product this page was for. Type and layout already earn
their keep by referencing the machine's own materials and by matching the
explain-then-convert shape this brief needs, so they stay as planned.
