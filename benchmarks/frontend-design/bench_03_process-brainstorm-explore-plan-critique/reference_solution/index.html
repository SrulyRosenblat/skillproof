The pressure comes from your arm, not a pump.

Aria is a fully manual lever espresso machine. No pump, no PID controller — you pull a spring lever by hand and build the 9 bar of pressure yourself, shot by shot.

Hand-pulled, every time

A spring lever means the shot's pressure curve is shaped by how you pull it — fast for a bright cup, slow and steady for a heavier one. The machine doesn't decide that for you.

Brass, steel, walnut

Brushed brass fittings and a matte black steel body sit under a walnut lever handle, shaped over several revisions to fit the palm through hundreds of pulls.

No electronics to fail

A passive thermosiphon boiler holds temperature without a PID loop. Fewer parts that can go wrong, and nothing to update.
